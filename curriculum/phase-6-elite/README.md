# Phase 6: Elite — Research & Capstones

> "The goal is to turn data into information, and information into insight." — Carly Fiorina

You've built the tree. Now you learn to grow it. Elite AI engineers don't just implement existing ideas — they read the literature fluently, identify gaps, form hypotheses, and build systems that advance the field. Phase 6 is where you transition from practitioner to contributor.

**Estimated time:** 9–12 weeks at 10–15 hours/week  
**Difficulty:** Expert  
**Prerequisites:** [Phase 5: The Realms](../phase-5-realms/README.md)

---

## What You'll Be Able to Do After Phase 6

- Read any ML paper and extract the key contributions in under 30 minutes
- Reproduce a research result from scratch, understanding where the original may have gaps
- Write a technical blog post or paper that communicates your own findings clearly
- Build a capstone project that demonstrates elite-level AI systems engineering
- Participate in peer review and give substantive feedback on others' work

### Module 2: Capstone Projects
**[→ Enter Module](02-capstone-projects/README.md)**

The culmination of Yggdrasil. You choose your project. You design the system. You build it and ship it.

**Project Options (choose one):**

| # | Capstone | Description | Skills Demonstrated |
|---|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| A | [Custom LLM Fine-tune](02-capstone-projects/A-custom-llm-finetune.md) | Fine-tune, evaluate, and deploy a domain-specific LLM with a complete evaluation suite | Phase 3–4 |
| B | [Distributed Training System](02-capstone-projects/B-distributed-training.md) | Train a model at scale, profile it, and write a detailed systems analysis | Phase 4 |
| C | [Production AI Agent](02-capstone-projects/C-production-agent.md) | Build a multi-agent system, evaluate it on a benchmark, and serve it in production | Phase 4–5 |
| D | [Safety Research Contribution](02-capstone-projects/D-safety-research.md) | Conduct original interpretability or red-teaming research and write a report | Phase 5 |
| E | [Open Research](02-capstone-projects/E-open-research.md) | Propose your own research question, design experiments, and document findings | All phases |

**Requirements for all capstones:**
- Public GitHub repository with clean, documented code
- Technical writeup (minimum 1500 words) explaining design decisions
- Quantitative evaluation against a baseline
- Peer review from at least one other Yggdrasil community member

## Elite Certificate

Learners who complete all 7 phases and their capstone project receive the **Yggdrasil Elite Certificate** — a verifiable GitHub commit history demonstrating completion of every phase, plus a community-reviewed capstone project.

This is not a piece of paper. It is a portfolio of working code, documented experiments, and peer-reviewed projects that demonstrates actual competence to any technical hiring manager.

## After Yggdrasil

Where do elite AI engineers go from here?

- **Contribute to Yggdrasil** — write lessons, review PRs, mentor new learners
- **Publish on arXiv** — the knowledge you've built is publishable
- **Apply to top teams** — Anthropic, OpenAI, DeepMind, major AI labs, and AI-first startups
- **Teach** — the best way to deepen understanding is to explain it to others
